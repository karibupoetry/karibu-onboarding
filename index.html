Module 1.1  ·  Origin

Where Karibu Poetry Began

Every movement has a starting point. Ours starts with a young woman who saw something real, felt it deeply, and decided to do something about it.

🇧🇮🇿🇦

Born in South Africa, rooted in Burundi

Our founder, Happiness Bujumbura, was born and raised in South Africa and is a descendant of Burundi. Growing up, she witnessed xenophobia directed at African foreign nationals — a prejudice rooted not in hatred alone but in a lack of exposure and understanding. As a creative, she turned to poetry as a way to articulate what others around her were living through. Not to be heard for her own sake, but because the experiences of those around her deserved language.

🌙

A Dream That Became a Direction

During a season of difficulty, Happiness had a dream about Karibu Poetry — what it could be, who it could reach, and what it could change. Three years later, she acted on it. That kind of clarity does not get ignored.

✈️

Nairobi, January 5th — The First Event

While passing through Nairobi, Kenya, Happiness hosted the very first Karibu Poetry open mic at Artcaffe Westlands — a space for poets, comedians, and singers to take the stage. What struck the audience was not just the quality of the event. It was that a young woman from a non-Swahili speaking background, someone who had not grown up in Kenya and was merely passing through, walked in and held the room with complete confidence. Many who were there left inspired to start their own creative endeavours. That is what a Karibu Poetry space does to people.

🌍

What "Karibu" Means — and Why It Matters

Karibu is Swahili for welcome. It is not just our name. It is the operating principle behind every event, every role, and every interaction. Everyone who steps into a Karibu Poetry space is welcomed exactly as they are.

What does "Karibu" mean?
Module 1.2  ·  Values

What We Stand On

Two words. Everything we do comes back to these.

Respect

Every person in our space gets the same warmth — first-time guest or famous poet walking past our door. Fame is not a currency here. Every person is.

Humility

We are always asking what we can give, not what we can get. A volunteer who is self-centred, dismissive of feedback, or unwelcoming to others is simply not a Karibu Poetry volunteer.

Module 1.5  ·  Code of Conduct

How We Carry Ourselves

These apply to every person on the Karibu Poetry team — not just the MC. We hold the space together.

Always ✅
  • Welcome every person with equal warmth, full stop
  • Give first-time performers extra care and encouragement
  • Dress expressively. Afrocentric and arts-inspired is very welcome
  • Snap fingers during performances. Applause comes after
  • Tag @karibupoetry and the venue on socials
  • Confirm the correct pronunciation of every performer's name
  • Save event dates immediately when they're announced
Never ❌
  • Beg or bully attendees for money under any circumstances
  • Make any guest feel uncomfortable or overwhelmed
  • Use classist, sexist, racist, or exclusionary language
  • Photograph anyone's poems or artwork without their consent
  • Hog the stage or make it about yourself
  • Treat high-profile guests differently from anyone else

Module 2.1  ·  Our Three Events

Three Distinct Experiences 🎪

Karibu Poetry curates three signature formats. Each one needs a different energy from you. Knowing the difference is non-negotiable.

🍵
Chai & Chill

Slow, healing, therapeutic. Guests write poetry and letters in a calm, low-pressure space.

Soft energy only. No chitchat. Set up flowers, rusks, grapes, tissues, paper, pencils, erasers, sharpeners. Sit with the guests — don't stand apart from them.

💌
The Stranger's Script

Speed-dating through co-written poetry. Strangers write line by line with each other every 6 minutes.

You are the wingperson. Encourage connections. Hype up the timid ones. Manage the bell. Keep the energy magnetic.

🎤
Open Mic Night

Vibrant and inclusive. Poets, musicians, comedians all on one stage.

Sign-up sheet on arrival. Support every performer. Use the lingo. Uphold the order. Nobody leaves that stage feeling unseen.

You've set up flowers, tissues, rusks, and writing paper. A guest arrives and tries to chat about their day. What kind of event is this and what do you do?

Module 3.2  ·  Earning Potential

How You Can Earn 💰

Karibu Poetry is a donation-based collective. All volunteer roles are unpaid. But there are real ways to earn as you grow and contribute.

🔗
Invite Code Commission

Every volunteer gets a personal invite code. When guests use it and you hit the event threshold, you earn commission. Paid monthly. Send your bank confirmation letter to karibupublishing@gmail.com to get set up.

🎤
MC Hourly Rate

After one year of consistent volunteer MCing, you earn a standard agreed-upon hourly rate per event. The patience is worth it.

📊
Head of KP Profit Share

30% of event profits go to the Head or Heads of KP. Unlimited earning potential as the platform grows.

Module 3.3  ·  What's Possible

This Actually Happened 🌟

You are not the first to walk through this door. Here is what happened when those before you showed up with a servant heart.

Wesley Akkid walked into a Karibu Poetry event in Nairobi with a microphone and a willingness to serve. He volunteered to MC and did it with such consistency, such presence, and such care for the space that he grew into a senior position. Not because he was the loudest person in the room. Because he was the most faithful.

Wesley Akkid  ·  Nairobi  ·  Volunteer MC to Senior Team Member

At one of our events in Nairobi at Bavaria Gardens, Westlands, Churchill — one of Kenya's most celebrated comedians — happened to be dining nearby. Something pulled him through the door. The energy of the room. He walked in, sat down, and performed a poem. He exchanged numbers with our team. He hadn't planned to be there. He just felt the welcome.

Churchill  ·  Bavaria Gardens, Nairobi  ·  A Night That Found Us

At our first Cape Town event, a young high school girl walked up to perform. She was the only performer that day. The event was short. But she delivered a poem on gender-based violence that stopped the room completely. When the footage went up on our TikTok, it went viral instantly.

Cape Town  ·  First Event  ·  One Voice That Stopped the Room
The Lesson

You don't need a big crowd, a big budget, or a big name to create impact. You need one person given a safe space to speak their truth. The rest takes care of itself. That is what Karibu Poetry builds — and you are now part of building it.
